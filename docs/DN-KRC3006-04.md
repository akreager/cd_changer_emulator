## 1. Purpose

This design note documents the requirements, decisions, and constraints for a custom breakout board intended to expose all 13 pins of the Kenwood CD changer interface connector (13-pin mini DIN) to accessible test points and pin headers. The board will be used during protocol capture and analysis phases of TP-KRC3006-01 and will remain part of the test bench for the duration of the reverse engineering effort.

---

## 2. Scope

This document covers the electrical requirements, mechanical considerations, KiCad design notes, fabrication plan, and mockup verification procedure for the breakout board. It does not cover firmware, protocol analysis, or the Arduino/Raspberry Pi integration architecture (see DN-KRC3006-01).

---

## 3. Background

The Kenwood KRC-3006 communicates with its companion CD changer via a proprietary synchronous serial protocol carried over a 13-pin mini DIN cable. To capture and analyze this protocol with a logic analyzer, all signal lines must be made accessible in a non-intrusive, electrically safe manner. A purpose-built breakout board provides a clean, repeatable solution and eliminates the need for improvised flying-lead connections that may introduce noise or reliability issues during capture sessions.

---

## 4. Design Requirements

| Number | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 1 | Board shall provide two female 13-pin mini DIN connectors (SDD-130J): J1 on the front side to mate with the head unit cable, J2 on the back side to mate with the changer cable. |
| 2 | All digital and control pins shall be individually accessible via 2.54 mm pitch female pin headers on J3 (11 pins). |
| 3 | Digital and control signals shall pass through between head unit and changer connectors with no active components in the signal path. |
| 4 | Audio channels (L CH, R CH) shall be routed to separate 3.5 mm TRS audio jacks — one for the head unit side (J4), one for the changer side (J5) — rather than passing through directly. Audio pass-through requires an external male-to-male 3.5 mm cable between J4 and J5. |

| 6 | Board dimensions shall be as compact as reasonably possible to minimize production cost. |
| 7 | Board shall accommodate SparkFun TOL-18627 logic analyzer male jumper leads without mechanical interference. |
| 8 | PCB shall be 2-layer FR4, standard 1.6 mm thickness. |
| 9 | Connector footprint(s) shall be verified against physical hardware using a 3D-printed mockup prior to fab submission. |
| 10 | The test bench shall be powered down prior to any reconfiguration of breakout wiring or logic analyzer connections. See Section 7. |

## 5. Connector Pinout Reference

The following pinout is drawn from the Kenwood factory service manual for the KRC-3006 and the KDC-CX85 CD changer. Signal directions are from the perspective of the head unit.

#### Pin 1 - REQ H (Active Low) | Head Unit → Changer
Request handshake output from head unit. Connects through a 100K series resistor to the µ-COM (LC72329-8839, IC6). Changer-side has an additional 100K series resistor (R34), giving ~100K+ total impedance. Head unit asserts this to initiate communication.

#### Pin 2 - GND
Audio ground. Believed to be internally connected to Pin 6 (D. GND) inside the head unit, based on schematic trace routing through CN5. Connecting to both pins is recommended to match the cable impedance the head unit expects from a real changer. Confirm continuity between Pin 2 and Pin 6 during Phase 1 testing.

#### Pin 3 - B.U. | Head Unit → Changer
Battery Unswitched — raw +12V always-on supply from vehicle battery via 3A fuse. No filtering or regulation on the head unit side. This rail also powers IC7 (TA8232H power amplifier) at 14.4V.

#### Pin 4 - ON | Head Unit → Changer
Changer connect/enable signal (CH-CON). Driven LOW by the head unit µ-COM to activate the changer. Active LOW per service manual — conflicts with some published documentation; verify polarity in Phase 1.

#### Pin 5 - MUTE | Changer → Head Unit
Mute request line (CH-MUTE). Driven by Q12 (DTC114YK NPN transistor switch) on the changer side. Active LOW — changer pulls this line low to request audio muting during disc changes or error conditions. Head unit side has 7.5K/10K/47K resistors and C103 (0.01µF) for noise filtering.

#### Pin 6 - D. GND
Digital ground. Believed to be internally connected to Pin 2 (GND) inside the head unit — the separate pin likely serves to reduce ground return impedance in the cable rather than to establish a true analog/digital isolation boundary. See Pin 2 notes.

#### Pin 7 - RESET | Head Unit → Changer
Reset pulse output to changer (CH-RST). Driven by Q13 (DTA114EK) on the head unit side. Direction and active polarity TBD — verify during Phase 1.

#### Pin 8 - R CH | Changer → Head Unit
Right audio channel, analog. Routed through IC3 (NJM4565MD dual op-amp) for buffering, then through IC2 (TC4066BF bilateral switch) to the head unit audio path. Audio will not pass until full protocol handshake succeeds and CH-CON is asserted. On this breakout board, R CH is routed to the 3.5 mm audio jacks (J4/J5) rather than passing through directly. See Section 6.

#### Pin 9 - REQ C (Active Low) | Changer → Head Unit
Request handshake output from changer to head unit. Driven through ~1K series resistor on the changer side. Relatively low impedance (~1K+) compared to the HU→Changer direction — fast edge speeds expected. Connected to the STB/strobe pin of the changer µ-COM, suggesting it may be hardware-managed during SPI transfers.

#### Pin 10 - DATA C | Changer → Head Unit
Serial data from changer to head unit. Driven through R28 (1K) on the changer side, received through R130/R131 (2.2K each) on the head unit side — ~5.4K total. Fast edges expected (~1mA drive current).

#### Pin 11 - DATA H | Head Unit → Changer
Serial data from head unit to changer. Driven through R145 (100K) on the head unit side, received through R24 (100K) on the changer side — ~200K total impedance. Only ~25µA signal current; expect significantly rounded edges at 125 kHz.

#### Pin 12 - L CH | Changer → Head Unit
Left audio channel, analog. Same signal path as Pin 8 (R CH) — routed through IC3 and IC2. On this breakout board, L CH is routed to the 3.5 mm audio jacks (J4/J5) rather than passing through directly. See Section 6.

#### Pin 13 - CLK (Active Low) | Bi-Directional
Synchronous clock, ~125 kHz, driven by the head unit µ-COM. Same impedance profile as DATA H — driven through R146 (100K), received through R25 (100K), ~200K total. Active LOW per schematic (overbar notation). Expect slow edges — at 30pF parasitic capacitance, τ ≈ 6µs, which is 75% of the clock period.

## 6. Audio Interception

The audio channels (Pin 8 / R CH and Pin 12 / L CH) are not routed as a direct pass-through between J1 and J2. Instead, they are broken out to two 3.5 mm TRS audio jacks mounted on the right edge of the board:

- **J4** — Head unit side audio. Tip = R CH (head unit), Ring = L CH (head unit), Sleeve = GND.
- **J5** — Changer side audio. Tip = R CH (changer), Ring = L CH (changer), Sleeve = GND.

The audio jacks use a standard 3-conductor (TRS) footprint with no switching contacts. When nothing is plugged in, the audio pins on both the head unit and changer sides float.

This topology enables three operating modes:

| Mode | Configuration | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Audio pass-through** | Connect J4 to J5 with a male-to-male 3.5 mm TRS cable. | Normal protocol capture with authentic changer audio reaching the head unit. Equivalent to a straight pass-through board. |
| **Audio intercept** | Connect monitoring equipment (oscilloscope, audio recorder, powered speakers) to J5. | Listen to or record the changer's audio output without routing it to the head unit. |
| **Audio injection** | Connect an external audio source to J4. Leave J5 disconnected or connected to monitoring equipment. | Feed test tones or music from a phone, DAC, or Raspberry Pi directly into the head unit's audio input path. Useful for verifying the audio path independently of changer protocol state. |

Audio pass-through requires the external cable. The board cannot function as a fully transparent sniffer without it. This is an intentional design decision — the separate jacks provide flexibility that a hardwired pass-through cannot.

## 7. Safety and Operational Procedures

The breakout board carries raw +12V battery voltage on the B.U. line (Pin 3 / J3) and 5V TTL-level digital signals. No series resistors are installed between the DIN connectors and the J3 test header. The existing source impedance on the bus — 100K+ on CLK, DATA H, and REQ H; lower on the changer-driven lines — provides some inherent current limiting, but an accidental short between B.U. and any signal pin could damage the head unit or changer.

**The test bench must be powered down before any reconfiguration of breakout wiring, logic analyzer connections, or audio cables.** This is the primary protection against accidental shorts and is a hard requirement — not a recommendation.

Procedure for connecting or reconfiguring the logic analyzer:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Power off the bench supply (disconnect 12V). |
| 2 | Verify no voltage on B.U. (multimeter or indicator LED, if installed). |
| 3 | Connect or rearrange logic analyzer leads and ground clips on J3 using male jumper wires into the female headers. |
| 4 | Connect or rearrange audio cables on J4/J5 as needed. |
| 5 | Visually inspect all connections. Confirm no jumper wires are bridging B.U. to adjacent pins. |
| 6 | Restore bench power. |

This procedure also applies when swapping between audio operating modes (pass-through, intercept, injection).

## 9. Design Decisions
#### J2 on back side
 The changer-side DIN connector (J2) is mounted on the back (B.Cu) side of the board, opposite J1 on the front. This places J1 and J2 through-hole pins on opposite copper layers with near-1:1 pin alignment, allowing the pass-through traces to be short direct runs rather than routing around one connector to reach the other. This significantly simplifies the layout and reduces trace congestion in the center of the board.
#### No series resistors on J3
 The Rev A/B requirement for 100 Ω series resistors on all test access points has been dropped. The head unit's existing source impedance (100K on CLK, DATA H, REQ H) provides inherent current limiting on the high-impedance lines, and the lower-impedance changer-driven lines (REQ C, DATA C at ~1K–5K) are tolerant of brief incidental contact. The primary short-circuit risk is the B.U. 12V rail, which no reasonable series resistor value would adequately protect against. The power-down reconfiguration procedure in Section 7 replaces the resistors as the protective measure.
#### Female headers on J3
 Female pin headers (sockets) are used for the test access points. Male jumper wires are inserted to connect the logic analyzer. This approach provides a more secure mechanical connection than clip leads on bare pins and reduces the risk of adjacent-pin bridging.
#### Separated audio path
 Audio channels are routed to 3.5 mm TRS jacks instead of passing through directly. This enables audio intercept and injection modes that support testing the audio path independently of the protocol state. The trade-off is that a male-to-male 3.5 mm cable is required for normal audio pass-through. See Section 6.
#### Non-switched audio jacks
 Standard AudioJack3 footprints (no switching contacts) are used. This simplifies the PCB layout and avoids the failure mode where a worn switching contact intermittently disconnects the audio path. The trade-off is that unplugged jacks leave the audio pins floating rather than defaulting to pass-through.
#### Board outline with cutouts
 The right edge of the board has two cutouts to accommodate the 3.5 mm audio jacks perpendicular to the board edge, keeping the overall width narrow. |

## 10. KiCad Design Notes

#### Connector footprint
SDD-130J 13-pin mini DIN, custom footprint constructed from connector datasheet dimensions. Verify pin 1 orientation before routing. J1 is mounted on the front (F.Cu) side; J2 is mounted on the back (B.Cu) side. Both use the same footprint. 
#### 3D model export
Export PCB layout as STEP (File → Export → STEP) for the mockup print. This keeps footprint geometry exactly synchronized with the fabricated board. 
#### Board outline
Defined on Edge.Cuts layer. 22 × 80 mm overall with 2 mm corner radii and right-edge cutouts for J4/J5. 
#### Test points
J3: 1×11 female pin header, 2.54 mm pitch, component side. 
#### DRC
Run DRC and resolve all errors before generating Gerbers. Minimum trace width ≥ 0.15 mm, clearance ≥ 0.15 mm for JLCPCB standard process. 
#### Gerber output
Use KiCad Plot (File → Plot) with JLCPCB layer naming. Export: F.Cu, B.Cu, F.Mask, B.Mask, F.Silkscreen, B.Silkscreen, Edge.Cuts, Drill file.

## 12. Mockup Verification Procedure

A 3D-printed mockup of the PCB shall be produced from the KiCad STEP export prior to submitting Gerbers. The purpose is to verify that the 13-pin mini DIN connector footprint matches the physical connectors before incurring fabrication cost.

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Export PCB layout as STEP from KiCad. |
| 2 | Print at 1:1 scale on home FDM printer. 0.2 mm layer height; no supports needed for a flat board mockup. |
| 3 | Test-fit a physical 13-pin mini DIN connector into the printed footprint holes. |
| 4 | Verify pin pitch, overall body fit, and mounting hole alignment (if any). |
| 5 | If fit fails: correct footprint in KiCad and repeat from step 1. |
| 6 | If fit passes: document pass with photograph and proceed to Gerber generation. |
| 7 | Mockup will be completed after connector arrival + 1 day (hard gate for fab submission). |
