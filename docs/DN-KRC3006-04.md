## 1. Purpose

This design note documents the requirements, decisions, and constraints for a custom breakout board intended to expose all 13 pins of the Kenwood CD changer interface connector (13-pin mini DIN) to accessible test points and pin headers. The board will be used during protocol capture and analysis phases of TP-KRC3006-01 and will remain part of the test bench for the duration of the reverse engineering effort.

---

## 2. Scope

This document covers the electrical requirements, mechanical considerations, KiCad design notes, fabrication plan, and mockup verification procedure for the breakout board. It does not cover firmware, protocol analysis, or the Arduino/Raspberry Pi integration architecture (see DN-KRC3006-01).

---

## 3. Background

The Kenwood KRC-3006 communicates with its companion CD changer via a proprietary synchronous serial protocol carried over a 13-pin mini DIN cable. To capture and analyze this protocol with a logic analyzer, all signal lines must be made accessible in a non-intrusive, electrically safe manner. A purpose-built breakout board provides a clean, repeatable solution and eliminates the need for improvised flying-lead connections that may introduce noise or reliability issues during capture sessions.

---

## 4. Design Requirements

| Number | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 1 | Board shall provide a female 13-pin mini DIN connector to mate with the stereo's existing cable. |
| 2 | All 13 pins shall be individually accessible via 2.54mm pitch pin headers. |
| 3 | Board shall be pass-through: stereo-side and changer-side connectors shall be electrically connected with no active components in the signal path. |
| 4 | Signal lines shall be labeled with their pin number per the factory service manual pinout. |
| 5 | Board dimensions shall be as compact as reasnably possible to minimize production cost. |
| 6 | All exposed signal access points shall include series resistors (100 Ω recommended) to protect against accidental shorts during probing. |
| 7 | Board shall accommodate SparkFun logic analyzer clip leads without mechanical interference. |
| 8 | PCB shall be 2-layer FR4, standard 1.6 mm thickness. |
| 9 | Connector footprint(s) shall be verified against physical hardware using a 3D-printed mockup prior to fab submission. |

## 5. Connector Pinout Reference

The following pinout is drawn from the Kenwood factory service manual for the KRC-3006 and the KD-CCX85 CD changer. Signal directions are from the perspective of the head unit.

| Pin | Signal Name | Direction | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | GND | — | Signal / chassis ground |
| 2 | DATA OUT | HU → Changer | Serial data from head unit to changer (TX) |
| 3 | DATA IN | Changer → HU | Serial data from changer to head unit (RX) |
| 4 | CLOCK | HU → Changer | Synchronous clock, ~125 kHz, driven by head unit |
| 5 | VCC | HU → Changer | +12 V supply from head unit to changer |
| 6 | AUDIO L+ | Changer → HU | Left channel audio, positive (balanced) |
| 7 | AUDIO L− | Changer → HU | Left channel audio, negative (balanced) |
| 8 | AUDIO R+ | Changer → HU | Right channel audio, positive (balanced) |
| 9 | AUDIO R− | Changer → HU | Right channel audio, negative (balanced) |
| 10 | MUTE | HU → Changer | Mute signal (active low) |
| 11 | SENSE / BUSY | Changer → HU | Changer busy / status line |
| 12 | RESERVED | — | Reserved / not connected (verify with SM) |
| 13 | RESERVED | — | Reserved / not connected (verify with SM) |

> **Note:** Pins 12 and 13 designations are tentative. Verify against factory service manual before routing. Do not assume NC — scope all pins during initial capture phase.

## 6. KiCad Design Notes

| Topic | Note |
|---|---|
| **Footprint source** | 13-pin mini DIN footprint will be constructed manually from connector datasheet dimensions. Verify pin 1 orientation before routing. |
| **3D model export** | Export PCB layout as STEP (File → Export → STEP) for the mockup print. This keeps footprint geometry exactly synchronized with the fabricated board. |
| **Board outline** | Add board outline on Edge.Cuts layer.  |
| **Test points** | Use 2.54mm pitch pin headter, female socket, for all test access points. Place on the component side with 2.54 mm spacing to accommodate logic analyzer clips. |
| **Series resistors** | 100 Ω 0402 SMD resistors on all signal lines (not power, not audio). Place in-line between connector pin and breakout header. |
| **DRC** | Run DRC and resolve all errors before generating Gerbers. Minimum trace width ≥ 0.15 mm, clearance ≥ 0.15 mm for JLCPCB standard process. |
| **Gerber output** | Use KiCad Plot (File → Plot) with JLCPCB layer naming. Export: F.Cu, B.Cu, F.Mask, B.Mask, F.Silkscreen, B.Silkscreen, Edge.Cuts, Drill file. |

## 8. Mockup Verification Procedure

A 3D-printed mockup of the PCB shall be produced from the KiCad STEP export prior to submitting Gerbers. The purpose is to verify that the 13-pin mini DIN connector footprint matches the physical connectors before incurring fabrication cost.

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Export PCB layout as STEP from KiCad. |
| 2 | Print at 1:1 scale on home FDM printer (work printer as backup). 0.2 mm layer height; no supports needed for a flat board mockup. |
| 3 | Test-fit a physical 13-pin mini DIN connector into the printed footprint holes. |
| 4 | Verify pin pitch, overall body fit, and mounting hole alignment (if any). |
| 5 | If fit fails: correct footprint in KiCad and repeat from M-01. |
| 6 | If fit passes: document pass with photograph and proceed to Gerber generation. |
| 7 | Mockup will be completed after connector arrival + 1 day (hard gate for fab submission). |
